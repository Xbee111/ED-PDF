Mobile Verification Centre

Mobile devices, such as smartphones, continue to excel in the 21st century due to the constant improvements of mobile technology. Advances in smartphones, such as increased computing power, improved device to-device communication and the option of installing additional third-party applications, have led to a sharp rise in their popularity. This popularity, combined with the extensive adoption of smartphones by the general public, has now drawn the attention of mobile malware developers. On popular platforms, such as Android, malware have grown exponentially since the detection of the first mobile Trojan horse in 2010. To prevent potentially malicious malware from entering the military environment, a verification process, similar to Google’s Bouncer, is recommended.

The development of a verification centre, which conducts security audits focusing on mobile capabilities within a military environment, is proposed to improve confidence by users that the device together with the software has not been compromised. The verification centre would consist of different components that include the auditing of mobile applications, evaluating physical mobile devices and providing a secure environment from where the mobile capabilities are made available to the operators.
